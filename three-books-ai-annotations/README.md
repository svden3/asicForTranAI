# Three Books AI Annotations for AGI Era

## Overview
AI-assisted annotations and analysis of three foundational texts for the AGI era:
1. **Sun Tzu's Art of War** (孙子兵法) - Strategy
2. **Zizhi Tongjian** (资治通鉴) - Historical governance
3. **The Bible** - Philosophy and ethics

Using NotebookLM and Claude agents for deep analysis relevant to AI governance, strategy, and ethics.

## What to Add Here
- **Annotations**: AI-generated insights and commentary
- **Cross-References**: Connections between texts
- **AGI Applications**: How ancient wisdom applies to AI
- **NotebookLM Sources**: Structured knowledge bases
- **Claude Agents**: Multi-agent dialogue prompts

## Methodology
- **NotebookLM**: Create knowledge bases from source texts
- **Claude Agents**: Multi-agent discussions between different perspectives
- **Cross-Reference**: Find patterns across traditions
- **AGI Application**: Extract principles for AI era

## Key Themes

### Sun Tzu (孙子兵法)
- Strategy without conflict
- Information asymmetry
- Adaptation and positioning
- **AI Application**: Alignment strategies, capability dynamics

### Zizhi Tongjian (资治通鉴)
- 1300 years of Chinese governance
- Pattern recognition in history
- Failure modes of institutions
- **AI Application**: Long-term AI governance, institutional design

### Bible
- Ethics and moral frameworks
- Wisdom literature (Proverbs, Ecclesiastes)
- Prophetic tradition
- **AI Application**: AGI ethics, existential responsibility

## Annotations Process
1. **Source Text**: Classical text in original + translation
2. **AI Analysis**: NotebookLM + Claude deep reading
3. **AGI Lens**: How does this apply to AI governance/ethics?
4. **Synthesis**: Universal patterns across traditions
